# Albert Camus: The Philosopher of the Absurd

Albert Camus (1913–1960) was a French-Algerian philosopher, author, and journalist. He was awarded the [Nobel Prize in Literature](https://www.facebook.com/Classicsliteratures/posts/on-december-10-1957-albert-camus-almost-refused-the-nobel-prizewhen-albert-camus/122265358862071158/) in 1957 at the age of 44, making him one of the youngest recipients in history.

## Core Philosophy: The Absurd

Camus is best known for his development of **Absurdism**. He defined "the Absurd" as the conflict between the human search for meaning and the "unreasonable silence" of the universe.

### Three Responses to the Absurd

According to Camus in _The Myth of Sisyphus_, humans typically face the Absurd in three ways:

1.  **Suicide:** A rejection of existence, which Camus condemned as a "confession that life is too much."
2.  **The Leap of Faith (Philosophical Suicide):** Turning to religion or transcendentalism to find artificial meaning.
3.  **Revolt:** The proper solution, where one accepts the Absurd and continues to live defiantly and passionately despite it.

## Major Works

Camus explored his philosophy through both fiction and essays:

| Title                  | Year | Genre | Key Theme                                                         |
| :--------------------- | :--- | :---- | :---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _The Stranger_         | 1942 | Novel | The indifference of the universe through the character Meursault. |
| _The Myth of Sisyphus_ | 1942 | Essay | The philosophical introduction to Absurdism.                      |
| _The Plague_           | 1947 | Novel | Human solidarity in the face of suffering and death.              |
| _The Rebel_            | 1951 | Essay | An examination of revolution and metaphysical rebellion.          |

## Notable Quotes

> "In the midst of winter, I found there was, within me, an invincible summer."

> "The only way to deal with an unfree world is to become so absolutely free that your very existence is an act of rebellion."

## Legacy

Camus remains a central figure in 20th-century thought. Though often grouped with **Existentialism**, he famously distanced himself from the label, particularly to distinguish his views from those of [Jean-Paul Sartre](https://www.reddit.com/r/askphilosophy/comments/1gp6oz3/who_to_read_first_sartre_camus_or_nietzsche/).
